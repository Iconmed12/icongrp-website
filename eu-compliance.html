Why This Matters

If Your Paperwork Isn't Ready,
Europe Won't Buy.

From 30 December 2026, every shipment of cocoa or timber entering the EU must be traced to the exact farm or forest and proven deforestation-free since 2020, with a filed Due Diligence Statement.

Miss it and you lose market access. Shipments are held. Contracts are cancelled. Buyers move to suppliers who have their documentation in order.

We make sure you don't. From farm mapping through to filing on the EU system, we handle the entire compliance chain so your goods keep clearing customs and your buyers keep buying.

What Non-Compliance Costs You
Shipments blocked at the border
Goods without a valid Due Diligence Statement cannot enter the EU market.
Contracts lost to compliant rivals
European buyers will simply source from suppliers whose paperwork is ready.
Penalties and reputational damage
Operators face significant fines and public listing for non-compliance.
Years of relationships undone
Once a buyer switches supplier, winning them back is far harder than staying compliant.

Which Rules We Cover

Two regulations are live now. A third is coming. We cover all three.

Live

Cocoa

EU Deforestation Regulation

Full traceability from farm plot to shipment, with deforestation-free verification and DDS filing for every consignment entering the EU.

Live

Timber

EU Deforestation Regulation

Chain-of-custody documentation, harvest legality evidence and geolocation of every forest concession or plot supplying your product.

Coming

Oil & Gas

EU Methane Regulation

Methane emissions measurement, reporting and verification obligations for producers and importers supplying the EU market.
