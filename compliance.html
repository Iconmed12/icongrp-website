Why This Matters

The world needs Congolese cobalt.
And it needs to prove it is clean.

The DRC produces around 70% of the world's cobalt, the metal at the heart of every electric-vehicle and phone battery.

Under the EU Batteries Regulation, any company placing batteries on the European market must prove their cobalt is responsibly sourced, traceable, and free of child labour. Fail, and you lose access to the market.

That proof cannot be assembled from a desk in Europe. It can only be built where the cobalt is mined, by people who can visit the sites, speak the languages and be trusted by the communities. That is exactly where we work.

Assurance, Not Extraction

Six services, delivered on the ground, that turn a supply chain into a defensible compliance position.

Mine-Site Monitoring

Independent checks on conditions, safety and child-labour risk, with photographs, structured data and honest reporting.

Traceability on the Ground

Artisanal (ASM) Formalisation
