Leadership

The people accountable for the work.

ICONGRP is led by a team drawn from clinical practice, regulated delivery, public sector procurement and international operations.

JM

Joel Mbala

Founder & Managing Director

Joel founded ICONGRP and leads the group across its care, workforce, consulting and technology divisions.

He began his career in recruitment before moving into project delivery, where he managed a portfolio of twenty care homes and led programmes valued at over £1m. He served as Head of Projects for a Glasgow-based organisation, leading the streamlining of its Netherlands operations, and later as Senior Bid Manager, writing tenders, leading compliance and delivering bids to deadline.

Joel holds a Bachelor of Laws. He directs the group's technology portfolio, including the Cana suite of care management, workforce and procurement software.

Bachelor of Laws Bid & Tender Management Programme Delivery Technology Portfolio

Queen Sengo

Head of Care & Community

Queen is a Registered Mental Health Nurse with the Nursing and Midwifery Council.

She leads ICONGRP's Care and Community division and provides clinical oversight across the group's health and social care work with local authorities, care providers and community organisations.

Registered Mental Health Nurse NMC Registered Clinical Governance
DS

Daniella Shogbamimu

Director of Delivery

Daniella is a qualified Scrum Master and Agile delivery manager.

She has led multi-million pound programmes and transformation initiatives across London Market insurance, banking, transport and recruitment, working with insurers, syndicates and brokers in tightly regulated environments.

At ICONGRP she is accountable for delivery governance across every division, from bid management and consulting through to workforce solutions and care contracts.

Certified Scrum Master Agile Delivery Regulated Environments Programme Transformation

Michael Ozumba

Director of Growth & Partnerships

Michael spent over five years as a Business Analyst in the NHS.

That experience gives him direct insight into how public sector health organisations define requirements, evaluate suppliers and reach purchasing decisions.

He leads growth and strategic partnerships at ICONGRP, working with public sector bodies, care providers and commercial partners.

NHS Business Analysis Public Sector Procurement Strategic Partnerships
